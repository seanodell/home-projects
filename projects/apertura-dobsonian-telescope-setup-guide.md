## Description

Quick-reference field guide for setting up, collimating, and using the Apertura Dobsonian Telescope (AD8 / AD10 / AD12). Covers everything from unpacking through first-light observing so you can get going without re-reading the full manual every time.

**Owner's manual:** `manuals/apertura-dobsonian-telescope.pdf`

## Notes

- The base and optical tube ship in two separate boxes. Once assembled the first time, the base stays together — you only need to separate the tube from the base for transport.
- The telescope uses a Newtonian reflector design: light enters the open end, bounces off the primary mirror at the bottom, then off a small secondary mirror near the top, and into the focuser / eyepiece on the side.
- Collimation (mirror alignment) is the single biggest factor in image quality. Check it every session.
- Included eyepieces: 30mm (2" format, low power) and 9mm (1.25" format, high power). Always start with the 30mm.
- The 35mm extension tube is needed to reach focus with low-power eyepieces. Insert it into the focuser first, then the eyepiece into it.
- The onboard fan (battery-powered) accelerates cool-down of the primary mirror. Use it.

## Phase 1 — Transport and Place

- [ ] Carry base housing by the handle to observing site
- [ ] Carry optical tube separately — grip by the tube, not the focuser or finderscope
- [ ] Choose a location away from streetlights and buildings, with a wide open sky view
- [ ] Place base on level ground, feet down
- [ ] Set optical tube into base cradle — silver side bearings rest in the curved cutouts on the left and right panels
- [ ] Orient the tube's metal seam toward the handle (front) of the base
- [ ] Silver side bearing flat faces point up

## Phase 2 — Attach Accessories

- [ ] Slide the 8x50 right-angle finderscope into the dovetail bracket on the tube; tighten thumbscrew
- [ ] Insert the 2"-to-1.25" adapter into the focuser if using 1.25" eyepieces
- [ ] Insert the 35mm extension tube into the focuser (needed for the 30mm eyepiece to reach focus)
- [ ] Insert the 30mm eyepiece (start low power)
- [ ] Connect the fan power pack to the rear fan port; turn fan on

## Phase 3 — Collimate (Laser Method)

Do this every session. Takes 2-3 minutes once you know the drill.

**Safety: never let the laser beam hit your eye — directly or reflected off the mirrors.**

- [ ] Remove any eyepiece from the focuser
- [ ] Visual check — look into the empty focuser and confirm: secondary mirror appears centered and circular; primary mirror and its three edge clips are visible reflected in the secondary. If the primary reflection is off-center, gently rotate the secondary mirror assembly by hand (don't touch the mirror surface) until it's centered
- [ ] Insert laser collimator into the 2" adapter sleeve, then into the focuser; tighten thumbscrew
- [ ] Turn on laser collimator; rotate its target face toward the primary mirror end (bottom of tube)
- [ ] Hold a piece of paper in front of the open tube end — if no laser dot appears on the paper, the beam is hitting the secondary (correct)
- [ ] Peer carefully into the tube from the open end — the laser dot should land in the center of the small donut ring on the primary mirror
- [ ] If the dot is off-center on the primary: adjust the three small screws on top of the secondary mirror assembly until the dot is centered in the donut ring
- [ ] Now look at the laser collimator's target face — the return beam should hit the center hole of the target
- [ ] If it's off: go to the bottom end of the tube, loosen the large **white** locking knobs, then turn the large **black** adjustment knobs until the return beam centers on the target
- [ ] Lock the white knobs; fine-tune black knobs as needed — tightening the locks can shift things slightly
- [ ] Remove laser collimator; re-insert eyepiece

## Phase 4 — Thermal Equilibrium

- [ ] Leave the telescope outside with the fan running for 30-60 min before serious observing (longer if the temperature difference from storage is > 20°F)
- [ ] Keeping the scope in a garage or shed between sessions reduces cool-down time

## Phase 5 — Align Finderscope

Only needed the first time or if it gets bumped.

- [ ] Insert the 30mm eyepiece (with 35mm extension tube if needed for focus)
- [ ] Point the telescope at a large, distant, stationary daytime target at least 250 yards away (billboard, antenna, etc.)
- [ ] Focus the main eyepiece using the focuser wheels — use the large wheel for coarse, the small 1/10-speed wheel for fine
- [ ] Look through the finderscope — focus it by loosening the lens cell (half turn), pulling back the locking ring, then turning the lens cell until sharp; re-tighten the locking ring
- [ ] Adjust the two black nylon thumbscrews on the finderscope bracket until the finderscope crosshairs center on the same object visible in the main eyepiece (the silver spring knob doesn't need adjustment)

## Phase 6 — Observe

- [ ] Allow 30 min for eyes to dark-adapt; avoid looking at lights (phone, car headlights)
- [ ] Use a red-lens flashlight for any tasks in the dark
- [ ] Start with the 30mm eyepiece (low power, wide field) to find targets
- [ ] Center the target, then swap to the 9mm eyepiece (high power) for detail
- [ ] Use the moon filter (1.25") when viewing the moon — it's painfully bright without it
- [ ] Track objects by gently nudging the tube — the Earth's rotation will drift objects out of view, faster at high magnification
- [ ] Star-hopping: find a known bright star or constellation, then scan toward your target using a star chart or app

## Focuser Reference

| Control | Function |
|---------|----------|
| Large wheel | Coarse focus |
| Small (1/10) wheel | Fine focus |
| Tensioning screw | Adjusts drag on the drawtube — increase for heavy eyepieces |
| Locking screw | Locks focuser position (under tensioning screw — must be backed out for full travel) |
| Compression screws | Secure the eyepiece in the focuser via brass compression ring |

## Eyepiece Quick Reference

| Eyepiece | Format | Use |
|----------|--------|-----|
| 30mm | 2" | Low power, wide field — finding targets, deep-sky objects |
| 9mm | 1.25" | High power — planets, moon detail, double stars |
| Moon filter | 1.25" | Dims the moon to comfortable brightness |
| 35mm extension tube | 2" | Insert between focuser and low-power eyepieces to reach focus |

## Teardown and Storage

- [ ] Remove eyepieces; cap them or store in the eyepiece tray
- [ ] Remove finderscope if transporting (leave the bracket attached)
- [ ] Place plastic tube cover over the open end of the optical tube
- [ ] Wipe condensation off the outside of the tube with a dry soft cloth — do **not** wipe the mirrors or lenses; let them air dry
- [ ] Lift the optical tube out of the base for transport
- [ ] Store indoors or in a garage / shed (not in a hot car)
- [ ] Never touch, rub, or wipe the primary or secondary mirrors — a thin layer of dust is normal and harmless. If cleaning is truly necessary, use only an optical lens brush and bulb puffer
